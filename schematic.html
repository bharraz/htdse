Package structure

Two tiers: core/ is the universal engine (true for any target-vs-reality problem); submodules/ are concrete physics primitives built on it. Each file: its role, its central equation, and why it lives in that tier.

Workflow — comparing an ideal gate to real pulses + a σz error

Every step: what you do, why the physics needs it, how in the package — and which layer of the hierarchy you are working in.

1
Write the targetyou work in: terms → group
WHAT
the ideal Hamiltonian you want the qubits to feel, HtargetH_{\text{target}}Htarget​
WHY
it is the reference every deviation below is measured against
HOW

2
Build the realized model — the same gate, as actually runyou work in: groups (swap by name)
WHAT
your real pulse envelopes Ω(t)\Omega(t)Ω(t) drive the qubits, plus the hardware error (a stray σz\sigma_zσz​ shift): Hreal(t)=Hdrive[Ω(t)]+ε σzH_{\text{real}}(t)=H_{\text{drive}}[\Omega(t)]+\varepsilon\,\sigma_zHreal​(t)=Hdrive​[Ω(t)]+εσz​

HOW
